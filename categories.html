Explore Our Movie Blogs

Below are our blogs in relation to examples of the connection between Japanese Culture and Intercultural Communications.

"Fast and Furious - Tokyo Drift" By: Maddie Chamblin

Tokyo Drift serves as a cinematic exploration of intercultural communication, illustrating the challenges and adaptations faced by an American protagonist immersed in Japanese culture. The film highlights key concepts such as high-context communication, cultural adjustment, and the negotiation of identity within a collectivist society.

"Spirited Away" By: Vanessa Comulada

Spirited Away bridges cultures through its stunning animation and heartfelt story, offering a powerful reflection on identity, empathy, and the beauty of intercultural understanding.

"Tompopo" By: Taryn Ferguson

Tampopo uses humor and storytelling to explore cultural values around food, community, and identity in Japan. The film highlights intercultural communication by showing how Western and Japanese customs blend and clash, especially through characters who challenge traditional roles and expectations around eating, gender, and hospitality.

"Lost in Translation" By: Kiya Grivalsky

Lost in Translation shows the challenges of intercultural communication through the experiences of two foreigners in Japan. The film explores how language barriers, unfamiliar customs, and different ways of expressing emotions can lead to feelings of confusion and loneliness. It also highlights how shared understanding can still be found across cultures through nonverbal communication and human connection.
